# Open Sandbox

The textbook gives you three stable reference points:

- `p = 0.5` keeps the distribution center-heavy
- `p = x` spreads mass across the full range
- `p = x^3 / (x^3 + (1-x)^3)` pushes mass toward the edges

Everything else you try in Stochast lives somewhere between those anchors or bends away from them in a new direction.

## What To Try First

Use the full sandbox when you want to:

- soften the positive-feedback curve and look for intermediate behavior
- introduce asymmetry and bias one side of the histogram
- increase or decrease `frame stride` to inspect playback density
- compare how agent count and step count change clarity without changing the rule itself
