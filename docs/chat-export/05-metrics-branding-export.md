# Metrics, Branding, And Export Extract

## Summary
This document captures the Gemini chat material related to the shareable layer of the product: metrics, exportability, naming, and the “scholar-marketing” framing.

## Core Metrics Proposed In The Chat
Gemini proposed three launch metrics:
- Bifurcation Score / Polarity
- Time to Lock / Convergence
- Gini Coefficient / Inequality

Gemini's intended interpretation was:
- Gaussian-like outcomes score low on bifurcation
- Uniform-like outcomes score around the middle
- edge-heavy outcomes score high on bifurcation

Relevant source:
- `R7`

## Export Concept
The user wanted a shareable PNG with the formula and resulting distribution.

Gemini extended that into a “Trading Card” concept:
- dark background
- neon histogram
- visible raw formula
- visible metrics
- subtle site watermark

## Scholar-Marketing Framing
Gemini described the growth angle as “scholar-marketing”:
- the product should appeal to developers, CS students, and math-oriented users
- the tone should be interesting rather than salesy
- shareable outputs should show off unusual distributions and formulas

Relevant source:
- `R7`

## What Was Carried Forward
These ideas directly informed the current plan:
- the three launch metrics
- the exportable PNG artifact
- the dark, technical export aesthetic
- the non-salesy, technically interesting tone

## What Was Not Carried Forward Literally
These ideas were mentioned but are not implementation commitments:
- specific Reddit posting tactics
- a “Star on GitHub” CTA as a launch requirement
- social strategy details beyond having a shareable export
